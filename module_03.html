Module 3: How do we tell data-driven stories?

You are developing a presentation for members of your state legislature outlining childhood obesity rates across ethnicities in your state. You are considering data for the last 10 years in your presentation.

Consider how you would tell the story with data to this group of policy makers.

Activity 1

Activity Objective

Consider the components of a data story. How does this scenario map to those components? What narrative structures might you consider utilizing? Why?

Elements of data stories

Remember the elements of data stories that you might want to utilize. Begin with the end in mind.

Audience

Who are you speaking to?

  • Are they internal? External?
  • Are they an expert? A novice?
  • What do they care about?
  • Why are they there?

Give Context to Issues

Consider utilizing these types of narrative structures:

  • Narrate change over time
  • Start big and drill down
  • Highlight contrasts
  • Profile the outliers
  • Start small and zoom out
  • Dissect the factors
  • Explore the intersection

Solution

Begin with the end in mind. Consider starting by understanding your solution:

  • What should the audience takeaway?
  • Is there a specific call-to-action?
  • What behaviors should change?
  • What is the expected result from the change?

Activity 3

Consider Grade 3 reading proficiency by race in Delaware’s school districts from 2015 to 2021.

Compose a data visualization for a policy maker, school administrator, or member of the public that encapsulates the best practices discussed throughout today’s presentation.

Data is sourced from Delaware’s Open Data Portal.

Data

Data Dictionary

The following fields are included in the dataset:

Field Description
School Year School year for which record is applicable. For example, 2016 = school year which ended in June 2016.
District Code Number representing each School District. Statewide data rows will have “0” in this column.
District Full name of the School District. Statewide data rows have “State of Delaware” in this column.
School Code Number representing each School within the school district. Statewide or Districtwide data rows will have “0” in this column.
Organization Full name of the Organization which is the School if School Code is given in the row. Districtwide data rows give full name of School District. Statewide rows give “State of Delaware” in this column.
Assessment Name Statewide summative assessment name. Valid values include: ACCESS – EL Annual ACCESS assessment; DCAS – Delaware Comprehensive Assessment System; DCAS-Alt1 – Delaware Comprehensive Assessment System – Alternate Assessment; DeSSAALT – Delaware System; SAT11 – SAT assessment; SBAC – Smarter Balanced Assessment Consortium
Content Area Statewide summative assessment subject (or content area). Valid values include: CMP – EL Annual ACCESS Composite Score; ELA – English Language Arts/Reading; ESSAY – Essay Component of SAT assessment; MATH – Mathematics; SCI – Science; SOC – Social Studies;
Race Represents the race/ethnicity of the unique group of students within a school/district.
Gender Represents the grade level of the unique group of students within a school/district.
Grade Represents the grade level of the unique group of students within a school/district.
SpecialDemo Represents the special population status of the unique group of students within a school/district.
Geography Represents the geography of the unique group of students within a school/district. Geography can have the following values: ALL – all geographies combined
SubGroup Names the unique group of students within a school/district described by the combination of Race, Gender, Grade, SpecialDemo, and Geography.
RowStatus Indicates whether the aggregate data in this row has either been REDACTED or REPORTED. If redacted, certain data has been hidden to comply with state and federal privacy laws. For more information, contact the Delaware Department of Education.
Tested The number of students who completed the statewide summative assessment listed with a valid score
Proficient The number of students who earned a proficient achievement level. This is typically considered as 3 or above.
PctProficient The percent of students who earned an achievement level considered proficient. This is the number of students who earned a proficient achievement level divided by the number of students who tested.
ScaleScoreAvg The average scale score for the specified group of students.
